  857      General and Technology-specific Techniques 
  858 
  859/General techniques/ describe basic practices that apply to all 
  860technologies. /Technology-specific techniques/ apply to a specific 
  861technology. 
  862 
  863Some success criteria do not have technology-specific techniques and are 
  864covered only with general techniques. Therefore, both the general 
  865techniques and the relevant technology-specific techniques should be 
  866considered. 
  867 
  868Publication of techniques for a specific technology does not imply that 
  869the technology can be used in all situations to create content that 
  870meets WCAG 2.0 success criteria and conformance requirements. Developers 
  871need to be aware of the limitations of specific technologies and provide 
  872content in a way that is accessible to people with disabilities.

  913      Testing Techniques 
  914 
  915Each technique has tests that help: 
  916 
  917  * 
  918 
  919    authors verify that they implemented the technique properly, and 
  920 
  921  * 
  922 
  923    evaluators determine if web content meets the technique. 
  924 
  925The tests are only for a technique, they are not tests for conformance 
  926to WCAG success criteria. 
  927 
  928  * 
  929 
  930    Failing a technique test does not necessarily mean failing WCAG, 
  931    because the techniques are discrete (that is, they address one 
  932    specific point) and they are not required. 
  933 
  934  * 
  935 
  936    Content can meet WCAG success criteria in different ways other than 
  937    W3C's published sufficient techniques. 
  938 
  939  * 
  940 
  941    Content that passes the sufficient techniques for a specific 
  942    technology does not necessarily meet all WCAG success criteria. Some 
  943    success criteria have only general techniques, not 
  944    technology-specific techniques. 
  945 
  946  * 
  947 
  948    The content must be accessibility supported 
  949    <http://www.w3.org/WAI/GL/UNDERSTANDING-WCAG20/conformance.html#uc-accessibility-support-head> 
  950    for the content's users. Some sufficient techniques require browser, 
  951    assistive technology, or other support that some users might not have.
